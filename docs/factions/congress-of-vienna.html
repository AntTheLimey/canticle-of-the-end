Congress of Vienna

Diplomatic Assembly

Leadership: Metternich (Austrian representative), Talleyrand (French), Castlereagh (British), and others

Territory: Vienna and surrounding regions

Overview

The Congress of Vienna is the diplomatic assembly reorganizing Europe after the Napoleonic Wars. It brings together the major powers and their representatives for negotiations that will reshape the continent’s political order.

Campaign Function

The Congress provides the social and political backdrop for Vienna Chapter 3:

Diplomatic Cover: The presence of international delegations provides cover for suspicious activity and travel.

Social Access: Major society events (receptions, balls, salons) provide opportunities for intelligence gathering and infiltration.

Political Leverage: Diplomatic status allows certain characters (like Harcourt) to move freely and access restricted areas.

Intelligence Resources: Diplomats and their staff provide sources of information about Vienna’s happenings.

Key Attendees Relevant to Campaign

  • Prince Metternich (Austria): Chief architect of the Congress, political powerhouse
  • Talleyrand (France): Seasoned diplomat, connected to British intelligence
  • Castlereagh (Britain): British Foreign Minister
  • Various nobility and military officers: Provide social contacts and intelligence sources

Masquerade Event (August 8)

A major social event where party members and Order contacts converge. Major William Thurner (Austrian Order operative) will be present as a contact point.

Geopolitical Importance

The Congress’s deliberations shape European political dynamics that will persist through the campaign’s timeline, including decisions about Poland (Warsaw), the Ottoman Empire (trade routes to India), and colonial holdings.

[!info] Keeper Note Use the Congress as atmospheric backdrop and as a source of social opportunity. NPCs attending Congress events can be either intelligence sources or obstacles. The diplomatic gathering creates plausible reasons for suspicious activity to be overlooked.
