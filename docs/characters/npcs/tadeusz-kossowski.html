Description

Kossowski is a Polish academic known to Albin_Herzfeld as a peer within the Aeternum_Choir’s fellowship. He appears in Herzfeld’s unsent letter (Handout 3 of Session_12_Plan) as the sole addressee Herzfeld trusts without reservation. The letter was unfinished and never sent: the ink was still tacky when recovered.

Nothing else about Kossowski is established. His existence in the record is entirely a function of Herzfeld’s need to confide in someone as the Vienna operation collapsed around him.

Background

Herzfeld wrote to Kossowski because he needed to set down what he knew while he was still capable of setting it down clearly. This framing implies Herzfeld was aware of his own deteriorating state: the sleeplessness, the pressure of the accelerated ritual timeline, the accumulation of operational losses he catalogued in the letter. Adler captured. Trautmannsdorff turned. The party closing in.

Kossowski is described as “the only member of our fellowship whose judgment I trust without reservation.” The letter treats him as a peer, not a superior. Herzfeld does not write to him for instructions. He writes to him to be understood.

Kossowski is located in Krakow. His institutional affiliation is not specified, but Herzfeld addresses him as a professor, which suggests a university post. The Jagiellonian University in Krakow is the historically plausible institution for this period, though this is not stated in the source document.

<!-- UNVERIFIED: Jagiellonian University affiliation — plausible inference from period context, not established in source. Needs Keeper confirmation before use. -->

Motivations

Kossowski’s motivations are entirely unknown from the source document. He is a recipient, not a speaker. The letter that reveals his existence was never sent, so his current state of knowledge about the Vienna operation is, as of the recovery of the letter, unchanged from whatever it was before Herzfeld began writing.

[!info] Keeper Only Because the letter was unsent, Kossowski does not know that the Vienna operation failed. He does not know Herzfeld’s fate. Whatever he believes about the state of the Choir is based on his last contact with Herzfeld before the assault on the University. This is potentially significant: a party that chooses to pursue Kossowski as an intelligence lead will find a man who is either still expecting a report of success, or who has received indirect word through other Choir channels. The Keeper should decide which before the party traces him.
