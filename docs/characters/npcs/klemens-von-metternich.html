Overview

Prince Klemens Wenzel von Metternich is the Austrian Foreign Minister and the most powerful political figure in Vienna. He is the chief architect and host of the forthcoming Congress of Vienna, which will reshape the map of post-Napoleonic Europe. Metternich controls the Geheimpolizei through his office at the Ballhausplatz and maintains a vast intelligence network.

Campaign Role

Metternich is not a direct antagonist or ally but a political landscape feature — the gravity well around which Viennese power orbits. His significance to the party:

  • Metternich’s writ (Session 10): The party obtained a writ from Metternich’s office (via Harcourt’s diplomatic channels) that recovered their confiscated materials from the Polizeidirektion, including the De_Vermiis_Mysteriis.
  • Political cover: Harcourt’s diplomatic status as British observer to the Congress provides the party with indirect protection through Metternich’s interest in maintaining good relations with Britain.
  • The Geheimpolizei complication: Inspektor Vogel reports through the Geheimpolizei chain to Metternich’s office. Metternich himself is likely unaware of Vogel’s Brotherhood connections, but the institutional link means Brotherhood influence reaches into the state security apparatus.
