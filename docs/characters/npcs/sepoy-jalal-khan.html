Description

The youngest, newest, and most frightened of the three Thuggee impostors in Fort_William. Twenty-two years old, three months embedded in the Bengal Engineers. Replaced the genuine Jalal Khan, a Muslim sepoy from Lucknow, within a month of the real man’s transfer.

A Hindu pretending to be Muslim. His Urdu is learned, not native, with a Bengali substrate that an investigator with Urdu/Hindi and a Hard INT roll might catch.

Background

Second-generation Thuggee. His father was Thuggee. His father’s father was Thuggee. He never had a choice. He has killed three men and vomited after each. His prayers are for the suffering to stop. He serves through fear of Ghosh, not through faith in Kali.

His role in the Fort: construction site intelligence. He serves under Renwick’s engineering detachment at the upstream embankment project, reporting construction details, progress, and troop dispositions to Ghosh.

Motivations

Survival. Fear. The weight of a tradition he cannot escape. Jalal Khan is proof that Ghosh’s system consumes its own.

Identification

  • Roster cross-reference against the journalist’s 30-name list reveals his name as still active
  • Accent check: Urdu/Hindi skill + Hard INT roll catches the Bengali substrate under learned Urdu
  • Behavioural tells: Flinches at loud noises on the construction site. Startles at wrong moments. Nervous in ways a seasoned sepoy would not be.

Interrogation

Jalal Khan is breakable.

He hesitates with the rumaal. His hands shake. He can be disarmed. If bound: he cries, reverts to prayer, fragments into Bengali.

Breaking methods:

  • Intimidation — produces bad intelligence (tells them what he thinks they want to hear)
  • Persuade — the key approach. Separate Ghosh from Kali. “Your master does not serve Kali. He serves something that would consume her.”
  • A female investigator — maternal psychology. He is twenty-two and terrified. Kindness undoes him faster than threats.
  • Isolation — silence and solitude undo him overnight

What He Eventually Gives Up

  • Ghosh’s name and the temple location
  • Deepak and Pratap Singh’s names
  • Other Thuggee faces in the city
  • Construction site details (embankment, cofferdam, engineering resources)
  • The core horror: screaming from the inner temple “that wasn’t human.” His father forbade asking. He has not slept through the night since.

What He Does Not Know

The true nature of the ritual. Dissolution. The Aeternum_Choir. Yog-Sothoth.
