Description

The oldest, most disciplined, least fanatical of the three Thuggee impostors in Fort_William. Thirty-eight years old, fourteen months embedded. Replaced the genuine Deepak Rao, a Marathi gunner from Bombay, within four months of the real man’s arrival, before he could form deep connections in the regiment.

Background

Professional Thuggee operative. His faith is real but controlled, the devotion of a soldier rather than a mystic. He kills because Kali demands it and because Ghosh commands it, and he has done both long enough that the acts carry no weight he cannot bear.

His role in the Fort: powder magazine access, artillery intelligence, quiet counter-intelligence observation. He watches who asks questions. He notes which officers spend time with which sepoys. He reports through the same chain of contacts as Pratap Singh, but independently. Ghosh does not let his operatives depend on a single channel.

Motivations

Duty. Faith expressed through obedience and discipline. Deepak Rao represents Ghosh’s twenty-year investment in infiltrating the most secure building in Calcutta. He is the culmination of a patient strategy.

Interrogation Resistance

Deepak Rao will not break. If cornered, he carries a rumaal for self-strangulation. If prevented from that, he uses a knife on himself (throat, efficient). If completely disarmed and bound, he enters death-silence: will not eat, drink, respond to questions, pain, or kindness.

The only possible break is a theological crisis: proving to him that Ghosh serves something that would consume Kali herself. This is extraordinarily unlikely and would require knowledge the investigators are unlikely to possess at the point of capture.

His silence is information. A man who dies rather than speak is a man whose master inspires absolute loyalty or absolute terror.

What He Knows

  • Temple layout, guard rotations
  • Names of all Thuggee operatives in the city and Fort
  • Kali Puja preparations, the signal to be ready
  • Powder magazine vulnerabilities

What He Does Not Know

Dissolution. The Canticle. The cosmic scope of the ritual. He serves Kali, not Yog-Sothoth.
