Description

Excellent English, excellent manners, impeccable military bearing. Polite, efficient, anticipates needs, solves problems before they become problems. The investigators will like him because he is good at his job.

The calm, helpful exterior is not entirely performance. He IS calm and helpful. He is also a man who strangles people with a silk cord and considers it prayer.

Background

Pratap Singh is not his real name. It is the name of the man he killed and replaced. The real Pratap Singh was a genuine havildar, well-liked, competent. The impostor has studied the man’s habits, relationships, and history well enough to pass casual inspection. He has been in place for eight months.

A true believer in Kali, not the Canticle, not dissolution, just Kali. Ghosh is his spiritual authority. He kills because Kali demands it. The rumaal is a sacrament. He is devout, not conflicted.

Function

  • Reports everything to Ghosh through a chain of contacts
  • Steers the investigators subtly: a “helpful” suggestion to visit this temple instead of that one, a route through the city that avoids the ghats where the journalist lives
  • Watches who they talk to. If they meet allies while he is present, those allies are immediately compromised
  • Waits for the moment of maximum vulnerability

The Betrayal

When it comes, it should be quiet. Not a dramatic reveal. The cord, from behind, at the worst possible moment. The man who carried their bags and opened their doors trying to kill one of them with the same patient efficiency.

Dramatic Irony

He is in the same regiment as Frome. Frome’s journal documents Thuggee killings. One of the impostors the investigators are hunting has been at their side the entire time. His name, or his regiment, appears on the list when they cross-reference it against Fort William records.

Motivations

Devotion to Kali. Obedience to Ghosh. The sacred duty of strangulation. He is not doing this for money, power, or survival. He is doing this because it is right. That conviction makes him patient, careful, and very difficult to detect.
