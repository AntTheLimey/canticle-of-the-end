Orphean Society

Cult Cell (Aeternum Choir)

Leadership: Unknown (cell leader)

Territory: London, Stonehenge

Members

  • Aeternum Choir

Overview

The Orphean Society is the London cell of the Aeternum Choir, operating as a respectable musical salon hidden within London’s upper-class society. The cell coordinates musical performances and social events designed to recruit new members and prepare for the Stonehenge ritual.

Status

DISRUPTED (Chapter 1). The investigators, working with the Order of St. Aelfric, successfully prevented the London ritual at Stonehenge during Chapter 1.

Ritual Segment

Segment I — Harmonic Framework: The foundational harmonic structure of the Canticle of Aeternity.

Campaign Role

The Orphean Society serves as the investigators’ introduction to the Aeternum Choir. Investigation in London reveals:

  • The existence of a global cult network
  • The pattern of musical ritual and recruitment
  • The international scope of the threat
  • Correspondence pointing to Lyon as the next active cell

Narrative Function

The successful disruption of the Orphean Society establishes:

  • The Order’s credibility and capability
  • The investigators’ competence and value
  • The reality of the supernatural threat
  • The necessity of coordinated global operations

The cell’s disruption also demonstrates that the Order is aware of and actively opposing the Choir — setting Choir paranoia levels for later chapters.

[!info] Keeper Note The Orphean Society is a completed chapter. It no longer has active operations. References to London success appear in later chapters as both Order confirmation of progress and Choir knowledge of opposition.
