Description

Late thirties. Fifteen years in India. Came as a clerk, bought his way independent within five, built the warehouse operation from nothing. Self-made.

Excellent company. Tells stories well, remembers names, donates visibly. Sits on the vestry committee. Attends Ashworth’s dinners as an equal. The Governor-General’s wife knows him by name. Built this reputation deliberately, because in Calcutta, reputation IS infrastructure.

The Edge

Something in the room with him that people feel. Conversations slightly too long, eye contact slightly too steady, laughter slightly too knowing. The man who finds out what bothers you and mentions it casually at dinner, watching your face.

Touches people when he talks. A hand on the arm, a grip on the shoulder that lasts one beat too long. Generous in ways that create obligation. Every kindness has a receipt. People leave a conversation with Surlish feeling slightly worse about themselves and unable to articulate why. Women work it out faster than men.

Background

Not cruel. Efficient. Doesn’t hate Hugh. Barely thinks about Hugh. Hugh is an obstacle occupying a role that would be more useful filled by someone Surlish controls. The squeeze is patient, impersonal, almost invisible: unfavourable warehouse assignments, adjusted fees, consignments that arrive late or short. At social events, patronising warmth (“Hugh’s doing marvellously, considering”) ensuring nobody takes Hugh’s complaints seriously.

The Cult Connection

Donates to the Mandir Mahakali because a consortium of Indian merchants he does business with asked him to. Helped suppress the journalist’s stories because one of those merchants said the articles were causing trouble, and Surlish leaned on the newspaper’s owner. Took five minutes. Never met Ghosh. The cult’s social shield, and has no idea.

The opium skimming happens in his warehouses. Indian staff include men connected to the Thuggee network, hired through the merchant consortium, never vetted.

Motivations

Commercial dominance. Social standing. Control. He optimises his environment the way he optimises his supply chain.
