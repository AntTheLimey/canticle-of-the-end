Description

A Prussian diplomat’s wife wearing magnificent emerald earrings. Encountered at Countess von Thun’s salon on the evening of 10 August 1814.

Background

Wife of a Prussian diplomat attached to the Congress delegation. Moves in the second-tier diplomatic social circuit. Regular guest at von Thun’s salon evenings.

Motivations

Social positioning within the Congress season. Standard diplomatic-wife objectives: information, connections, status.

Connections

  • Maria_von_Thun — salon hostess, social circle
  • Prussian delegation — through her husband

Session 11 — The Kaunitz Intelligence (10 August 1814)

At von Thun’s salon, Baronin von Kessel gossiped that the Generalfeldmarschall had not been seen for two days. This is significant intelligence that the party has not yet fully processed. Kaunitz’s absence from Vienna society coincides with the Brotherhood’s escalating preparations for the 15 August ritual.

[!info] Keeper Only The Kaunitz absence is a piece of intelligence hiding in plain sight. The party heard it as salon gossip. They have not yet connected it to the possibility that Kaunitz has withdrawn to the University or gone to ground in anticipation of the ritual. This should surface as a “wait, what did she say?” moment when the party begins strategic planning in Session 12.
