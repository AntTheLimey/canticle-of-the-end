Description

A Bengali fisherman who knows the Hooghly River intimately. His name is common in Bengali; it is also the month when the monsoon withdraws and river fishing resumes, October, the chapter’s timeframe.

Background

Kartik fishes near Baranagar. He has noticed things the British administration has not. Fish behave strangely near the temple ghat. Water hums at certain times. Nets tangle as if currents reversed. Among the boatmen it is known: the water near the old temple is wrong. Kartik stays clear after dark.

He wants his fishing grounds back.

What He Offers

Practical river geography: currents, channels, depth, where sound carries, how the tides work, which approaches to Baranagar are least observed from the ghats. Useful for climax logistics and navigation. He can provide discreet river transport.

His knowledge of the river’s acoustic anomalies is one of five independent paths to discovering the Hooghly’s role as the ritual’s transmission medium. The others: the marked PC’s compass, Roy’s 1812 letter, Renwick’s observations, and Tara.

Discovery Paths

  • Through Apu, who knows river people
  • Through Roy, whose documentation mentions river anomalies
  • Independent river investigation (asking around the docks)
  • Following the marked PC’s compass toward the river

Motivations

His livelihood. The river is his world and something is wrong with it. He does not understand the cause but he knows the effect. He will help anyone who can make the water right again.
