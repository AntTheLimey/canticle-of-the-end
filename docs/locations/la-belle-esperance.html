Overview

La Belle Espérance (The Beautiful Hope) is a British packet boat of French design, seized during the Napoleonic Wars and now put to peacetime use by the Royal Mail Service. A narrow-beamed and fast vessel with a graceful foremast and patched but proud mainsail bearing a faded Union Jack, it serves as the investigators’ passage from Dover to Calais at the opening of Chapter 2.

Specifications

  • Design: French-built, fast packet boat design
  • Ownership: Royal Mail Service (British)
  • Captain: Captain Thomas “Tom” Keene
  • Current Duty: Cross-Channel passenger and mail service between Dover and Calais
  • Condition: Well-maintained; sails are patched but functional

Capacity

  • Passenger Capacity: Approximately 15–20 passengers depending on cargo
  • Cargo: Mail, trade goods, passenger luggage
  • Officers/Crew: Captain Keene and approximately 4–6 crew members

Amenities

  • Deck Space: Open for passengers
  • Below-Deck: Cramped passenger cabins (spartans)
  • Galley: Basic food and drink
  • Facilities: Heads (ship’s toilets); limited washing facilities

Role in Campaign

La Belle Espérance appears in two contexts:

  1. Opening Scene (Dover Docks): Investigators board here; Captain Keene briefs them
  2. Calais Arrival: They disembark and encounter French authorities

The voyage is brief but establishes:

  • Practical sailing details
  • Captain Keene’s character
  • The transition from England to France
  • A moment of relative safety before the investigation begins

Keeper Notes

  • The crossing should take 4–8 hours depending on conditions
  • Captain Keene is the primary NPC during this sequence
  • Other passengers may provide color and minor complications
  • Do not extend the voyage unnecessarily; it is a transition scene
