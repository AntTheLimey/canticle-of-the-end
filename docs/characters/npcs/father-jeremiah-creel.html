Overview

Father Jeremiah Creel is an elderly fire-and-brimstone nonconformist preacher who accidentally stumbled upon one of the Orphean Society’s rituals. Captured and held in the sub-basement for over a month, he has endured sensory isolation and psychological torment.

Current Condition

Gaunt and half-mad from imprisonment, his eyes retain lucidity despite his fractured mental state. He alternates between fevered religious discourse and coherent testimony about the Society’s operations.

Key Information

If rescued and calmed:

  • Eyewitness testimony about ritual activities
  • Descriptions of cult members and their behavior
  • Knowledge of sacrifice victims and their fates
  • Timeline of his own capture and detention
  • Religious perspective on the supernatural phenomena

Roleplaying Hooks

  • Highly traumatized; requires careful, compassionate handling
  • Will resist rescue unless investigators invoke divine authority
  • Speaks in fevered allegory and biblical reference
  • Once calmed, becomes valuable informant despite mental fragility
  • May struggle with survivor’s guilt

Relationship to Campaign

Creel is a secondary captive whose rescue provides:

  1. Additional moral motivation for investigators
  2. Varied perspective on cult operations
  3. Potential longer-term NPC alliance (clergy contact)
  4. Atmospheric horror detail
