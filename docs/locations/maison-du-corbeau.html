Overview

Maison du Corbeau (House of the Raven) is the recently inherited estate of Lucien Goupil, located in the old ecclesiastical district of Lyon. A former canon’s house, it is architecturally suited to theatrical occultism and serves as the venue for the July 1st Masquerade Soirée—a key ritual event in the Aeternum Choir’s operations.

Location & Geography

  • District: Old ecclesiastical quarter of Lyon
  • Style: Former ecclesiastical property; retained gothic and religious architectural elements
  • Exterior: Crumbling, ivy-covered walls; gnarled trees lean over the property; carved gargoyles loom over moonlit grounds
  • Entrance: Black iron gates with “Maison du Corbeau” in gilt script
  • Approach: Gated courtyard accessible by carriage

Exterior Features

  • Gates: Wrought-iron lilies (pale, weathered)
  • Grounds: Moonlit gravel drive
  • Trees: Ancient, gnarled, skeletal
  • Gargoyles: Carved stone figures surveying the property
  • Lighting: Candles burn in every window; acrid, metallic scent rides the warm air

Interior Layout

Main Hall

  • Black Draperies: Draped extensively throughout
  • Chandeliers: Ten or more, flickering with candlelight
  • Elevated Dais: Where orchestra performs
  • Reception Area: Where guests are formally greeted

Music Rooms (Three)

The Crimson Chamber

  • Instruments: Harpsichord, soprano vocalist
  • Atmosphere: Draped in crimson silk
  • Music: Corrupted aria; part of ritual performance

The Violet Hall

  • Instruments: Low brass and woodwinds
  • Music: Atonal, hypnotic, nearly imperceptible at first
  • Effect: Induces strange moods in listeners

The Silver Salon

  • Instruments: Glass harmonicas
  • Music: Whispering overtones audible to trained listeners
  • Effect: Disturbing resonance that some hear more clearly than others

Library/Study

  • Contents: Strange scores, alchemical books, discarded drafts
  • Hidden Element: Folded note on floor hinting at midnight ritual: “Midnight. South Corridor. Show both tokens.”
  • Atmosphere: Cluttered, dim, dusty

South Wing / Ritual Chamber

  • Access: Hidden beyond false wall panel in South Corridor
  • Requirement: Two tokens (specially carved bone discs) required to enter
  • Interior: Black marble floors etched with musical and Orphic notation
  • Candelabra: Six candelabra with blue flame
  • Circle: Masked musicians arranged in circle; instrumentalists tuned to unnatural dissonances

Servants

All servants are disturbing:

  • Mute Footman: Child-sized, masked, silent, greets at gate
  • Child Servants: Approximately 10–13 years old, identically masked in white porcelain with black eyes and closed mouths
  • Dressed: Red livery, silent, unsettlingly well-trained

They serve:

  • Roasted quail with violet petals
  • Black pastries with bitter custard
  • Ink-colored wine
  • Champagne with gold leaf flakes
  • Blood-orange sorbet in delicate cups

Guest List

Approximately 30–40 masked guests from Lyon’s occult, artistic, and wealthy circles. Examples include:

  • Woman in clockwork owl mask, dressed masculine
  • Man with flayed-face mask, loudly laughing
  • Pair of lovers in dog and bitch masks, chained at throat
  • Young man with mirror mask, repeating “it is coming”
  • Hunched woman with cage-like mask containing paper birds

Key Elements

The Password

Entry requires speaking the password: “Rebirth through Dissonance”

The Tokens

Two tokens are required to enter the secret ritual chamber:

  1. Public token: Given at the door upon entry
  2. Secret token: Obtained through pickpocketing Comte Vallin, finding in library, trading with Victoire, or enticing a child servant

The Masques

Each investigator is provided with a unique masque:

  • Emma & Georgiana Wentworth: Sunburst gold and crescent moon silver (Apollo/Artemis pair)
  • Marina Garrick: Cracked white porcelain with silver wire, eye-shaped opal on forehead
  • Charlotte Thorne: Barn owl-style leather mask
  • Augustus Bolt: Blackened bronze clock face with broken gears and gold filigree

The Ritual

The secret ritual involves:

  • Harmonic Offering: Participants use bronze rods and ceremonial knives to contribute magic points and blood to a ritual basin
  • Unveiling of the Spawn: A slimy aperture opens in the floor, and a Formless Spawn (servitor of Tsathoggua) rises
  • Marking of the Chosen: Specific cultists receive burning sigils marking them irrevocably to the Choir
  • Child Sacrifice: A child is placed on an altar and killed with a tuning-fork blade, causing death by harmonic resonance

Role in Campaign

Maison du Corbeau is the venue for the Masquerade Soirée, a key ritual event where the investigators:

  • Encounter multiple cultists
  • Witness the power and horror of the Choir’s workings
  • Must decide whether to infiltrate the secret ritual or observe from the public soirée
  • Experience the first direct supernatural threat

Keeper Notes

  • The atmosphere should be theatrical but increasingly disturbing
  • Music is both beautiful and wrong; make this contrast clear
  • The child servants are intentionally unnerving; do not explain their origins yet
  • The ritual chamber is horrifying; SAN checks are appropriate
  • The Formless Spawn is not a combat encounter; it is an apparition

SAN Exposure

Investigators witnessing various elements should make SAN checks:

  • Seeing child servants (0/1)
  • Entering the ritual chamber (0/1)
  • Witnessing the Spawn (1/1D6)
  • Witnessing the child sacrifice (1D4/1D20)

Campaign Design Notes

Maison du Corbeau represents the Choir’s confidence and open practice of occultism. By July 1, they are past the point of hiding; they openly recruit, test, and mark new cultists. This signals the accelerating timeline toward August 15.

Campaign History

Chapter 2 — Lyon

The masquerade soirée took place as planned. The investigators hunted down invitations — dropped, pickpocketed, or given — donned their masks, and infiltrated the event. They witnessed the secret ritual in the hidden chamber: the Formless Spawn rising through the aperture, the child sacrifice by tuning-fork blade. The party observed everything and did not intervene. They left traumatized, carrying knowledge they could not put down.
