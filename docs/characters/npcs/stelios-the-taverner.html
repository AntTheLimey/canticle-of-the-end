Description

A barrel of a Greek in his late fifties, bald and bearded, forearms like cables, an apron stiff with years of spilled wine. Booms a welcome at every stranger and remembers every face. Runs a low waterside kapelio in the Frank quarter — a smoky single room with benches, a fiddle on a nail, and a back shelf of retsina, raki, and a date arrack that strips paint. Sailors of every flag drink here.

Background

Came to Alexandria off a Greek coaster thirty years ago and never re-shipped. The tavern is the harbour’s unofficial news exchange — who arrived, who sailed, whose cargo was interesting, which captain is hiring or fleeing. Stelios trades the news for custom and keeps the worst of it behind his teeth.

Motivations

  • A full room and a quiet one — he’ll throw a knife-puller out the door himself.
  • Gossip is his stock-in-trade; he gives it freely to good customers and shades it for coin.

Connections

  • The liquor-and-gossip stop on the Alexandria streets (see Session_03_Alexandria). A natural draw for Holt, Thomas, and Freddy.
