Laya Sampradaya Draft

Religious cult (Aeternum Choir cell)

Leadership: Acharya_Devendra_Ghosh

Territory: Mandir Mahakali, Baranagar Ghat, western bank of the Hooghly

Members

  • Havildar Pratap Singh
  • Naik Deepak Rao

“The Lineage of Dissolution”

Overview

A millennia-old teacher-to-student tradition predating the European cells by thousands of years. The name sounds like a legitimate Hindu spiritual lineage because it partly is one. The Laya Sampradaya operates openly from the Mandir Mahakali at Baranagar Ghat. Their concealment is not hiding but visibility: the British see a temple. They do not see what the temple contains.

Goals & Agenda

Complete the Cycle of Dissolution: the Aeternum_Choir’s fourth ritual segment. On Kali Puja night (October 25, 1814), svarita carriers embed among genuine funeral chanters at the cremation ghats. They don’t replace the death chants; they harmonize with them, inserting the Canticle’s frequencies into living religious practice. The yantra focuses the frequencies into the Hooghly through the temple’s foundations. At full power: physical dissolution of hundreds of mourners. A luminous slurry of dissolved consciousness flowing into the river.

If successful: The Cycle completes, bringing the Choir closer to the 5-of-8 threshold for the Grand Canticle.

Leadership

Acharya_Devendra_Ghosh — Absolute authority based on lineage and knowledge. Born to this tradition. POW 90, SAN 0 (functionally insane, operationally coherent), Cthulhu Mythos 35. Calm, learned, charitable, sincerely beloved by his community. The horror is philosophical: he means every word.

Members

~25-30 total:

Circle Number Role
Inner circle 5-7 Svarita carriers (singers with specific vocal mutation). Identified young, raised from childhood. Physically marked by practice dissolutions. They CRAVE dissolution.
Temple priests 8-10 Maintain the yantra and manage the public face. Know varying degrees of truth.
Outer circle ~10-15 Servants, ghat-workers, informants in river communities. Most believe they’re serving Kali.
Thuggee Variable Enforcement arm. Thuggee traditionally worship Kali. Ghosh uses them for disappearances and intimidation. They think they’re serving Kali. They are. They don’t know what Ghosh is building.

No cell structure needed. The temple IS the structure.

The Yantra

Not a machine. An architectural inscription: sacred geometric patterns carved into the temple’s inner sanctum (floor, walls, ceiling) over centuries. Functions like a tuning fork the size of a room. As svarita-enhanced chanting reaches the temple from the ghats, the yantra focuses it into a coherent frequency and feeds it into the river through the temple’s foundations, which sit directly on the ghat waterline.

Territory & Resources

  • Temple: Mandir Mahakali at Baranagar Ghat. Legitimate, well-endowed, openly functioning.
  • Funding: Temple donations (legitimate), wealthy Hindu merchant patrons (most unknowing). No covert funding needed.
  • The Hooghly: The river is the transmission medium. The temple’s foundations sit on the waterline.
  • Thuggee network: Enforcement, including three impostor sepoys inside Fort William.
  • Kaunitz: Ghosh’s window into the British world. Watches the social scene.

Concealment

Social visibility. Inverse of every European cell. The cult’s activities are indistinguishable from normal Hindu worship because they partly ARE normal worship, with something terrible woven through. The British don’t understand what they’re looking at. The merchant patron network (Baijnath_Mullick, Hawthorne_Surlish) provides an additional social shield without knowing what they’re protecting.

Cult Insanity

The inner circle are sane, or rather, they’ve passed through insanity into something else. They’ve experienced dissolution and found it transcendent. They WANT it. The horror is that they’re not wrong about the experience: it does feel like liberation. It also destroys you utterly.

Defector

Tara — Former svarita carrier. Priest’s daughter. Fled three years ago. Hiding in Calcutta’s margins. Ghosh considers her manageable. She carries the knowledge that makes the counter-ritual possible.
